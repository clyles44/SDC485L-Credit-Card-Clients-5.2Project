## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Credit Card Dataset using machine learning techniques to explore customer behavior, credit approval patterns, model optimization, and business decision-making through scenario analysis.

The project was completed across five phases:

1. Data Collection and Preliminary Analysis
2. In-Depth Data Exploration and Preparation
3. Basic Machine Learning Implementation
4. Advanced Modeling and Optimization
5. Scenario Analysis and Business Insights

## Dataset

Credit Card Dataset

The dataset contains customer demographic and financial information including:

* Income
* Age
* Expenditure
* Credit Reports
* Account Activity
* Credit Card Ownership
* Dependents
* Credit History Length

## Scenario Analysis Results

Three business scenarios were evaluated:

### Economic Stress

* Lower income
* Higher expenditures
* Increased credit report activity

Approval Rate: 71.97%

### Improved Financial Health

* Higher income
* Lower expenditures
* Reduced credit report activity

Approval Rate: 73.48%

### Customer Growth

* Longer account history
* Increased account activity
* Additional credit card relationships

Approval Rate: 75.38%

## Sensitivity Analysis

Income and expenditure were evaluated to determine their impact on model predictions. Results indicated that the optimized model remained relatively stable under moderate financial changes, demonstrating robustness and reliability.

## Key Findings

* Customer Growth produced the highest approval rate.
* Economic Stress produced the lowest approval rate.
* Income and expenditure influence approval decisions but are not the sole determining factors.
* Scenario analysis provides valuable insights for business decision-making and risk management.
